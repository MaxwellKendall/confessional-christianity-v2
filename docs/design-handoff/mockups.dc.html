Notes on the first question of the Shorter Catechism
Thomas Ainsworth · July 8, 2026
Westminster Shorter Catechism · Q. 1
Q. What is the chief end of man?
A. Man’s chief end is to glorify God, and to enjoy him forever.

Most catechisms open with a question about God or Scripture. The Shorter Catechism opens, instead, with a question about us — and answers it with a claim so bold it is easy to read past: that our end, our purpose, is not obligation but enjoyment.

Joy Is Not the Reward

The answer does not say we glorify God in order to enjoy him, as though joy were a wage paid out afterward. The two verbs sit side by side, undivided. Glorifying and enjoying are one motion, not two.

In thy presence is fullness of joy; at thy right hand there are pleasures for evermore.
Psalm 16:11

This is why the catechism can afford to begin here, before a single doctrine has been stated. Everything that follows is an answer to a question this one already assumes: how, then, shall we enjoy him?
